A Visit To The Beautiful City of Gembu, Taraba State

Reasons You Should Visit Gembu

  • Its currently the coldest place in Nigeria
  • Gembu is home to the largest tea farm in West Africa, and two of Nigeria's biggest tea companies buy their tea from there.
  • Most houses in Gembu are made from red soil, and some believe they are "bulletproof"
  • Gembu is home to dark brown cattle with large horns

Things you will see at Gembu

  1. The people of Gembu are known for their hospitality, warmth, and rich cultural heritage
  2. Popular drinks in Gembu include kunu, made from grains like sorghum, millet, and maize, and fura da nunu, a local yogurt made from cow milk and millet dough.
  3. Chappal Waddi Mountain: Nigeria's highest peak, with an elevation of about 2,419 meters.
  4. Mbamnga and Ndumyaji Cave: Features a footprint of an ancient warrior at Kabri, a horse footprint at Hienary, and the historic site of an ancient blacksmith.
  5. it is a quiet and ancient town with tourist attraction like big foots and a love garden
